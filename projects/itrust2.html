iTrust2

iTrust2 originated as a project for the CSC326 Software Engineering course in the Computer Science Department at NC State University. It is primarily maintained by Kai Presler-Marshall and Sarah Heckman. The goal of iTrust2 is to immerse students in software engineering practices through a substantial, relevant, and evolving application that incorporates security and privacy requirements. If you're interested in using iTrust2 for your software engineering course, please don't hesitate to get in touch with us.

iTrust2 is an electronic health records system (EHR) designed to empower patients to manage their health records and communicate with their healthcare providers (HCPs). Healthcare providers can use iTrust2 to document office visits, including basic health metrics, diagnoses, prescriptions, eye care, and pregnancy care. iTrust2 adheres to HIPAA regulations to ensure the security and privacy of patient records. Key Features

Prescriptions

Allows healthcare providers to manage prescriptions for patients, including creating, updating, and tracking prescriptions.

Diagnoses

Records and Manages diagnoses for patients, helping to keep track of their medical conditions and treatment plans.

Password Functionality

Enhances the security of the system by enabling users to change their passwords and ensuring that passwords meet security requirements.

Edit Demographics

Update patient demographics, ensuring that the information in the system is accurate and up to date.
